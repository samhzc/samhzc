Project: Artificial Eye
Aim and Purpose
Machines have become indispensable in the world we live in now, and it will be even more so in the future. CST, a creativity support tool, plays a big part in our daily life. Every CST is aiming for the same goal – to help humans to complete any creative task in every possible way, thus I designed a CST prototype called the Artificial Eye. The main purpose of this CST is to help visually impaired people to sense and measure their surroundings in a different and effective way and then to be able to build any kinds of kennels or catteries within the size of 2 meters for small animals such as dogs, cats, or chickens, etc.
Motivation
Safety is one of the most important sensations for every living creature in this world, including humans. There are lots of factors which could cause humans to feel safe or unsafe, the perception of the current environment is one of the main factors. “Compared to people with normal vision, those with vision impairment are at a higher risk for depression, anxiety, and other psychological problems (Kempen et al., 2012).” As this research reported, people who are visually impaired have more restrictions in their daily lives, thus I wish to design something that can reduce those restrictions and allow them to live safer, more comfortably and most importantly with the ability to be creative. The visually impaired people will be able to have a better grasp of information around them through the Artificial Eye, in which they will feel more confident, safer, decrease the possibility of encountering dangerous situations and increase in mental health. By giving them more safety and confidence, they could be more encouraged to express their ideas by creating something on their own. Can you imagine how many more inspirations, new concepts and wonderful ideas could be brought to the world if every visually impaired person had the ability to build and create everything as the same as non-visually impaired people? What if someone has strong imagination skills and plenty of creative ideas but has no way to show or express correctly to others just because that person can not see? I would not see all those talents gone wasted, thus the Artificial Eye I designed is not just for visually impaired individuals to be able to create, but also for the world to be more colorful and a better place which includes as many ideas, innovations from everyone in the world as it can.
How does the Artificial Eye work
The Artificial Eye is a wearable CST that can be worn on the hand. It emits ultrasonic waves to the desired direction with real time feedback which determines if there are objects in that direction. The feedback is received by the user in the form of different intensities of vibration according to the distance. By sensing that vibration, the user could know the estimated distance from the object without being able to see and then perform different actions when doing a creative job. For example, if the person is building a small house for the dog. He/she can use this tool to measure and estimate the distance that can not be reached or measured by arm, like the length between two walls of the dog house or the height from the roof to the floor. There are 3 levels to the vibration, the intensity of first level vibration is the strong and it is active if the feedback distance is within one meter; the intensity of the second level vibration is weak and it is active if the feedback distance is within the range of one meter to two meters; the vibration will stop when it reaches to third level which the feedback range is greater than two meters. This CST could help visually impaired people to accurately estimate one meter and two meters, then they can roughly estimate (with greater accuracy than estimating without this CST) any other distance within two meters range.

Future Works
By following the steps above, you should be able to replicate the Artificial Eye prototype that works exactly like the one I have described previously. However, due to limitations to the part on hand, this prototype is not the best solution for the desired function. There are a lot of improvements that could be made. For example, the servo motor can be replaced by a vibration motor to increase the vibration efficiency. Also we could add a sound module to increase the accuracy for the user to recognize the change in distance. Furthermore, we can replace the ultrasonic sensor with an IR sensor to increase the accuracy of measured distance, increase the range it can measure and also reduce the feedback time intervals. As well there are many improvements that can be made on coding. With this prototype as the foundation, I wish someday in future a complete product can be created that could change the lives for visually impaired people and also change the world no matter how subtle the changes are.
