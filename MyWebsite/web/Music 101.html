Music 101
Aim and Purpose
Music composition is often considered a challenging task for many people due to the complex musical concepts and technical skills required. To address this challenge, we have designed and implemented a Creativity Support Tool (CST) called “Music 101” using an Arduino Uno circuit board. The main goal of this CST is to provide a user-friendly platform that enables users to compose a short musical sequence by simply pressing buttons on an IR remote. The tool uses a buzzer to produce sound of specific notes and a LCD screen to display the notes being played. This report presents the design and implementation of Music 101 and evaluates its effectiveness in supporting music composition for novice users. The purpose of this CST is to simplify the process of music composition and make it accessible to a wider audience with varying levels of musical expertise. The Music 101 CST offers a new opportunity for people to explore their musical creativity and develop their musical skills in a fun and engaging way.
Motivation
As an undergraduate student, building a CST like “Music 101” can offer several motivations. For me, this project provides an opportunity to learn new skills and gain hands-on experience in electronics, programming, and prototyping. It also allows me to express my creativity through music composition and explore the possibilities of using technology to support artistic expression. Moreover, this project can help me develop problem-solving skills that can be applied in various fields. For others, this project provides a simple and accessible way for anyone to create their own musical piece, without needing to have any formal training or access to musical instruments. I found the idea of being able to create music intuitively and spontaneously, at any time and in any place is very useful and very much needed in our society.

Related Work 4
One example of a project that uses microcontrollers to create interactive music experiences is the “Arduino Musical Instrument with Light Theremin” developed by Tdelatorre on ProjectHub [4]. In this project, an Arduino Uno is used along with a breadboard and various components such as a photoresistor and an LED to create a musical instrument that can be played by waving a hand over the photoresistor. The Arduino code reads the analog input from the photoresistor and maps it to a frequency for the tone generator. The LED is also controlled by the Arduino code to provide visual feedback.
Figure 4 :Theremino: a theremin made with Arduino[4] (https://www.hackster.io/413422/theremino-a-theremin-made-with-arduino-8631c6)
Related Work 5
Another one example of a project that uses microcontrollers to create interactive music experiences is the “Ultrasonic Piano” developed by Linndz [5]. This project involves using an ultrasonic sensor to detect the distance between the user’s hand and the sensor, and then mapping that distance to different notes on a piano. The project uses an Arduino Uno board and a breadboard, along with various components such as the ultrasonic sensor and piezoelectric speakers. The Arduino code reads the distance measured by the sensor and converts it into a corresponding frequency to generate the appropriate note on the piano. The project also includes a potentiometer to control the volume of the sound output. Overall, the Ultrasonic Piano is a unique and creative way to use microcontrollers to create an interactive musical experience.

Prototype design and implementation
The following parts should be prepared in advance:
  • UNO R3 Controller Board x1
  • USB Cable x1
  • Buzzer x1
  • LCD 1602 Module x1
  • Potentiometer x1
  • Breadboard x1
  • Female-to-Male x1
  • Breadboard Jumper Wire x25
  • 9V Battery with Snap-on Connector Clip x1
  • Resistor x5
Step 1: Add a buzzer to Your Circuit
  • Connect buzzer’s short leg(-) connect to ground(GND) by black wire
  • Connect buzeer’s long leg(+) connect to any digital number on the UNO R3 Controller Board by red wire
Step 2 : Add a Potentiometer to Your Circuit
  • Connect one terminal of the potentiometer to 5V (VCC) using a red wire.
  • Connect the other terminal of the potentiometer to ground (GND) using a black wire.
  • Connect the middle terminal of the potentiometer to A2 on the UNO R3 controller board using a purple wire.
Step 3:Add the LCD Screen to Your Circuit
  • Connect the positive (anode) lead of the LCD to a resistor.
  • Connect the other end of the resistor to 5V (VCC) on the breadboard using a red wire.
  • Connect the negative (cathode) lead of the LCD to ground (GND) on the breadboard using a black wire.
  • Connect the contrast (V0) pin on the LCD display to a potentiometer.
  • Connect the R5 pin on the LCD display to analog pin 5 (A5) on the UNO R3 controller board.
  • Connect the Enable (E) pin on the LCD display to analog pin 4 (A4) on the UNO R3 controller board.
  • Connect the DB4 pin on the LCD display to digital pin 6 on the UNO R3 controller board.
  • Connect the DB5 pin on the LCD display to digital pin 7 on the UNO R3 controller board.
  • Connect the DB6 pin on the LCD display to digital pin 8 on the UNO R3 controller board.
  • Connect the DB7 pin on the LCD display to digital pin 9 on the UNO R3 controller board.
  • Connect the GND pin on the LCD display to ground (GND) on the breadboard using a black wire.
  • Connect the ACC pin on the LCD display to 5V (VCC) on the breadboard using a red wire.
TIPs:
To turn on the LCD screen, use a potentiometer. Adjust the potentiometer to turn up the LCD screen. Once the LCD screen is turned on, you should see the text print out.
Step 4: Connect the IR Receiver Module
  • Connect G of IR Receiver Module to Breadboard Ground.
  • Connect R of IR Receiver Module to Breadboard Power.
  • Connect Y of IR Receiver Module to Master Uno Board register 13.

Electronics Choice & Code Logic
The IR sensor was used to receive signals from an IR remote, which was selected for its simplicity and user-friendly interface. The buzzer was used to produce the sound of the specific note, while the LCD screen was selected to show the note being played. The code logic developed for the project was motivated by the need to produce sound and display the note being played on the LCD screen when a button on the IR remote is pressed, allowing the user to keep track of the notes played and form a short musical sequence.
Accomplish the Creative Task
To accomplish the creative task of composing a musical piece, the user needs to press the buttons on the IR remote to produce the desired notes, which will be displayed on the LCD screen. The user can keep track of the notes played and form a short musical sequence. The project provides a simple and fun way for users to experiment with music composition, particularly for beginners or those looking for a creativity support tool.
Critique
This is a self-evaluated strength and limitations of this project.
Strength
The Music 101 prototype developed using an Arduino Uno circuit board has several strengths and limitations. One of the main strengths is that it provides an easy and interactive way for users to create short musical sequences by pressing buttons on a remote control. The LCD screen displays the note being played, and the buzzer produces sound of different notes based on the button pressed. The code is well-organized and easy to understand, making it accessible for beginners in electronics and programming.
Limitations
However, the prototype also has some limitations. The musical sequences that can be created are limited to the specific notes pre-programmed in the code, and there is no option to adjust the duration or timing of the notes played. Additionally, the use of an IR remote control limits the range of control and may require line-of-sight communication. The use of a single buzzer also limits the ability to create complex musical pieces with multiple notes and harmonies. Overall, the Music 101 prototype is a good starting point for those interested in exploring the intersection of electronics and music, but it has limitations that may make it unsuitable for more advanced musical applications.
Future work
Music 101 is an exciting project that has the potential to revolutionize the way people create and play music. However, there are several ways in which the project could be extended and improved to provide even greater functionality and usability. One direction in which Music 101 could be expanded is by adding more functionality to the remote control. Right now the remote control allows the user to select pre-programmed musical sequences. However, additional buttons could be added to allow the user to adjust the tempo or to select different instruments or change the volume. This would enable users to experiment with different sounds and create more complex musical compositions. Another potential direction for the project is to incorporate machine learning techniques. This requires a lot more knowledge on programming languages and algorithms. By analyzing user input and musical patterns, the tool could generate its own musical sequences. This would allow people to collaborate with the tool and create new musical works that they may not have thought of on their own. Also, this tool could be connected to a computer or other external device, allowing for more advanced musical composition and production. Improvements could also be made to the user interface. Adding a touch screen or other input method would make the tool more intuitive and user-friendly. Users could easily select and manipulate different sounds, adjust the tempo, and control other aspects of the tool’s functionality. Most importantly, this prototype could be miniaturized to create a more portable and user-friendly tool for users on the go. This would allow users to take the tool with them wherever they go, enabling them to create and play music anywhere and at any time. There are numerous ways in which the Music 101 project could be extended and improved to provide even greater functionality and usability for users. Incorporating machine learning techniques, adding more functionality to the remote control, improving the user interface, and miniaturizing the prototype are just a few possible ways for future development. With continued innovation and development, the Music 101 project has the potential to transform the way people create and perform music.
